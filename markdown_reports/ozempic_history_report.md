# The History of Ozempic: Development, Use, and Key Milestones

## Discovery and Development of Semaglutide

Ozempic’s active ingredient, semaglutide, is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. Semaglutide was developed by Novo Nordisk, building on earlier work with the GLP-1 analog liraglutide. Semaglutide was discovered in the early 2000s through a process of modifying the GLP-1 molecule to increase its half-life, allowing for once-weekly dosing.

## Clinical Trials and Regulatory Approval

Semaglutide underwent extensive clinical evaluation through the SUSTAIN program (Semaglutide Unabated Sustainability In Treatment of Type 2 Diabetes), a series of large, international phase 3 trials. These studies demonstrated significant improvements in glycemic control and weight reduction among participants with type 2 diabetes.

Ozempic (semaglutide) was first approved by the FDA in December 2017 for the treatment of type 2 diabetes in adults. The European Medicines Agency (EMA) followed with approval in February 2018.

## Launch and Initial Indications

Upon launch, Ozempic was indicated as an adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes. It was not approved for use in type 1 diabetes or pediatric populations at launch.

## Key Scientific Studies

The pivotal SUSTAIN trials established Ozempic’s efficacy and safety. These studies showed superior HbA1c reduction and weight loss compared to placebo and some other diabetes medications. Cardiovascular outcomes from SUSTAIN-6 demonstrated a reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events in high-risk patients.

## Updates and Expanded Use

Subsequent research and regulatory submissions led to additional indications. In June 2023, the FDA expanded Ozempic's use to include reducing the risk of major cardiovascular events (e.g., heart attack, stroke) in adults with type 2 diabetes and known heart disease. Recent studies have also explored its benefits for patients with kidney disease and prediabetes.

## Impact and Comparison with Other GLP-1 Agonists

Ozempic has had profound impact on diabetes management thanks to its effectiveness in reducing both blood sugar and weight. Compared to earlier GLP-1 agonists (like exenatide and liraglutide), semaglutide offers improved glycemic control, superior weight reduction, and the convenience of once-weekly injections.

## Recent Developments, Ongoing Research, and Controversies

Ozempic’s popularity surged due to observed weight loss, prompting off-label use for obesity. This led Novo Nordisk to develop Wegovy, a higher-dose formulation of semaglutide specifically for chronic weight management. High demand has at times led to drug shortages.

Ongoing studies are investigating effects in broader metabolic and cardiovascular conditions, while debates continue regarding off-label use, side effects (such as gastrointestinal issues and rare risk of pancreatitis), and accessibility concerns.
