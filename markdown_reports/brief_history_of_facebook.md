# A Very Short History of Facebook

Facebook was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, along with his college roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes, while they were students at Harvard University. Initially designed as a social networking platform exclusively for Harvard students, it quickly expanded to other Ivy League universities and then to colleges and universities across the United States.

The platform opened to the general public in 2006, requiring only an email address to join. Facebook introduced key features such as the News Feed in 2006 and the Like button in 2009, which significantly increased user engagement. The company rapidly scaled, surpassing 1 billion active users by 2012.

Facebook went public in 2012, raising $16 billion in one of the largest tech IPOs. The company expanded its portfolio by acquiring Instagram (2012), WhatsApp (2014), and Oculus VR (2014), affirming its position as a leader in social media and technology. In 2021, Facebook was rebranded as Meta Platforms, Inc., reflecting a new focus on the "metaverse" and virtual reality technologies.

Despite its growth, Facebook has faced scrutiny over privacy, data use, and the spread of misinformation. Today, Facebook remains one of the world’s largest social networks, at the center of ongoing discussions about digital society, privacy, and innovation.
