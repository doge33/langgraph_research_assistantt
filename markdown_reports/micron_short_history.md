# Short History of Micron Technology

## Founding and Early Years
- **Founded:** 1978 in Boise, Idaho, by Ward Parkinson, Joe Parkinson, Dennis Wilson, and Doug Pitman.
- **Early Focus:** Initially, Micron concentrated on designing and manufacturing DRAM (dynamic random-access memory).

## Key Technological Milestones and Innovations
- **1981:** Released its first 64K DRAM.
- **1994:** Introduced the 16Mb DRAM.
- **2000s:** Expanded portfolio to include NAND flash memory, NOR flash, and advanced memory technologies.

## Major Business Expansions and Acquisitions
- **1996:** Micron went public (NASDAQ: MU).
- **2006:** Acquired Lexar Media, expanding into storage and consumer products.
- **2012:** Acquired Elpida Memory, making Micron a leading DRAM supplier.
- **2013:** Bought Rexchip Electronics, further boosting manufacturing capacity.

## Recent Developments and Current Status (as of 2024)
- **2020s:** Invested heavily in next-generation memory, including 3D XPoint and advanced DRAM/NAND.
- **2023:** Announced $15 billion investment in a new U.S.-based fab amid global supply chain reforms.

## Market Position and Industry Significance
- **Industry Role:** One of the largest memory manufacturers globally, competing with Samsung and SK Hynix.
- **Products:** Supplies memory for computers, mobile devices, automotive, and data centers.
- **Recognition:** Known for innovation in semiconductor memory and persistent expansion in high-growth markets.

**Summary:**  
Micron Technology, founded in 1978, has grown from a DRAM startup into a global leader in memory and storage solutions, marked by consistent technological innovation, strategic acquisitions, and a strong industry position as of 2024.
